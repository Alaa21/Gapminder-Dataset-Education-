def my_function(df,temp): 
    col=df.columns.tolist()
    col.pop(0)
    df_lx=pd.melt(df, id_vars =['country'], value_vars =col) 
    df_lx.rename(columns = {'variable':'Year','value':temp}, inplace = True)

Research Question 2 :¶

How countries are performing in education based on the literacy rate indicator?¶

Do top performing countries in education have high life expectancy?¶

In the following code:¶

We will try to find out how countries are performing in education based on the literacy rate. The following shows the steps to be followed in order to answer the question:

1:To choose the most recent year in the data (which is 2011)

2:To remove all missing values from the dataset.

3:To change the data type from decimal to integer, since if we will draw the histogram for decimals it will show many different values and it will not be visually clear which rate has the highest count of values.

4:To draw the bar plot, which based on count of values shows the distribution of literacy rate over the different countries.

5:To identify countries with the highest and lowest literacy rates, based on the .max() and .min() functions.

6:To remove all missing values in rate literacy and life expectancy and calculate the correlations between the two variables using the corr(method='pearson').

Limitations¶

1: Data was not suffieicient to prove the findings, many missing data for many countries. For instance the life expectancy data is only available for 184 countries.

2: There are no recent data, the recent numbers on life expectancy sts are on 2018

3: All data were pivoted.

4: Since there were a lot of missing data in some years or for some countries, it was difficult to find the correlation between two unrelated variables in the same year.

Conclusions¶

Key Findings:¶

1: Almost in 100 countries the GDP per capita is below 10k dollars.

2: Qatar is the country which had the highest GDP per capita (with almost 123000) in the world for 2019, and Somalia had the lowest GDP per capita (with only 631).

3: The data shows that there is a strong positive relationship between Years spent in education and GDP per capita.

4: Cuba, Moldova and Poland are on eone of the countries in the world which have 100% rate of literacy, where Mali is one of the countries which have the lowest literacy rate (with 46% Literacy rate).

4: The correlation coefficient shows a strong positive relation between Literacy rate, and Life expectancy.
